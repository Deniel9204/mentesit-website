Good IT operations is the kind you never notice: servers run, backups exist, updates land — and you get to focus on your own work. We provide that background so the gaps don't reveal themselves at 2 a.m. during an outage.

## What server operations covers

We take over your existing servers and systems, or set them up from scratch — on bare metal, virtual servers, or self-hosted cloud. Day-to-day operations means concrete things here:

- **Monitoring and oversight** — we continuously watch services, load, disk and certificates, with alerting that fires before your users notice a problem.
- **Backups and recovery** — we don't just back up, we regularly restore into a test environment, because a backup is only worth something once you've proven it restores.
- **Security updates and maintenance** — keeping the OS, runtime and dependencies current, in documented maintenance windows.
- **Logging and traceability** — so that after an incident you can actually say what happened and when.

## Typical situations

People reach out when a system already matters but has no dedicated team behind it. A few real cases:

- A web app or internal system is in production, but no one is watching it around the clock.
- You've outgrown the "a colleague half-maintains it" stage and need a reliable backstop.
- An inherited server with patchy documentation — order needs restoring before something breaks.
- An audit or a client expectation requires proper backups, monitoring and a real update process.

## How we work

We start with process, not passwords. We map out what runs, what's backed up where, what counts as critical — and turn that into a documented, transparent operational baseline. We tune monitoring so alerts actually mean something: few but important signals, not noise. We flag changes before they happen, agree on maintenance windows, and tell you afterwards what changed — in plain language, not error codes.

We've built and run large, continuously-running systems in production (e.g. insurance-sector platforms with many interconnected apps), so we know where things tend to snap under load — and what's worth securing in advance.

## Why us

We work with an aggressively self-hosted mindset: your monitoring, logs and backups can stay where you control them, not inside a third party's black box. That's a cleaner position for data privacy, and it doesn't tie you to a platform's pricing. We're a small team with a real person on the other end — you're writing to someone who knows your system, not to a ticket factory.

If there's a server or system you'd like a calmer backstop behind, send us a few lines about what runs today — on the first call we can already tell you concretely where you stand and what the next step is.
